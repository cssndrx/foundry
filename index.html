We have 10 years to make climate ideas happen. Fast.

In leaving Google engineering roles to take climate action, we (Cassandra Xia and Eugene Kirpichov) started the workonclimate.org community and have been doing pro-bono and non-profit work. For the last few months, we have been searching for non-obvious problems in the climate sector to build a for-profit venture. Coming in from tech many people work on carbon offsets, carbon accounting, and B2C sustainability — as a result, these are crowded spaces with many strong teams. 
‍
We now believe the main difficulty in the climate space is getting access to problems non-obvious to outsiders, e.g. reverse logistics, maritime shipping, low GHG refrigerants. As the Climate Foundry, we provide value by surfacing important and non-obvious problems from domain experts and building teams with the new tech talent entering the space. We are advised by ____. 

01.

Non-obvious ideas

We source ideas from climate experts

We call up, interview, and aggregate information from experts in decarbonizing industries because we believe the best ideas in climate are non-obvious, often spottable only by experts in the weeds of a specific domain. We transform this information into concrete projects using our network of experienced entrepreneurs. We share this information with you and invite you to participate.

02.

Sprints

Two month sprints

We bring together top notch players across business, climate, and tech to rapid prototype climate ideas in two month cycles. Each team commits to working full-time for two months towards building up the idea as a for-profit venture. We will support you with our network of experts, mentors, and investors.
